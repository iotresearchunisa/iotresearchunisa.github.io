Laboratory Overview

The Internet of Things Laboratory (IoTLab) is a central hub for innovative research and collaboration activities with national and international research institutes and companies on IoT system design and deployment. 46 workstations for supporting research and development activities.

The IoTLab is formed by researchers (professors, PhD students, and post-docs), together with several Mastersâ€™ students. Interdisciplinarity is a key principle of all research activities. The goal of the IoTLab is move from theory to practice.

The current research activities focus on IoT resource optimization, sensor networks, context-aware, IoT security, vurnerability analysis, penetration testing, edge and cloud computing for IoT, Tiny Machine Learning, Federated Learning for IoT, and data analysis. Applications include smart homes, smart grid, e-Health, smart cities, environment monitoring, intelligent transportation systems, smart agriculture, UAV systems, etc.
