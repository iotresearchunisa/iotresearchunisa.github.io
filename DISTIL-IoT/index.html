Scope & Rationale

The pervasive diffusion of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies is reshaping critical sectors such as healthcare, smart cities, industrial systems and critical infrastructures, generating unprecedented volumes of distributed data that hold enormous potential for intelligent, data-driven decision-making. However, the heterogeneous, resource-constrained and privacy-sensitive nature of IoT environments poses fundamental challenges to traditional centralized learning approaches, calling for novel distributed and collaborative intelligence paradigms that are secure, sustainable and reliable.

DISTIL-IoT 2027 focuses on the design, optimization and deployment of distributed learning frameworks — including Distributed Learning, Federated Learning, TinyML and related approaches — tailored to the specific demands of IoT ecosystems. Topics span communication-efficient and energy-aware training algorithms, privacy-preserving and robust mechanisms, handling of non-IID and heterogeneous data, fault-tolerant architectures and trust management, as well as the integration of distributed intelligence with edge computing, blockchain and digital twins.

The workshop also welcomes contributions on the assessment and improvement of quality attributes of intelligent IoT applications — security, privacy, reliability, energy efficiency, maintainability — that ultimately determine the trustworthiness of the resulting systems. Case studies, benchmarks, testbeds and interdisciplinary works bridging distributed intelligence with ethical, regulatory and environmental considerations are equally welcome.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

Federated & Distributed Learning

Communication-efficient and energy-aware training algorithms, split learning, swarm learning, decentralized and hierarchical architectures.

Security & Privacy

Privacy-preserving and robust learning, AI-driven detection and mitigation of threats, trust management, adversarial robustness.

Heterogeneity

Non-IID and heterogeneous data, model and device heterogeneity, interoperability among sensor networks, WLANs, cellular, satellite and wired infrastructures.

Energy Efficiency & Sustainability

Energy-aware IoT and TinyML architectures, optimization strategies, low-power distributed intelligence, trade-offs between efficiency and performance.

Fault Tolerance & Resilience

Fault-tolerant distributed learning architectures, self-learning and self-healing mechanisms, resilient inference at the edge, explainability and human interaction.

Enabling Technologies

Edge and fog computing, blockchain, digital twins, Quantum Computing, Agentic AI, lightweight cryptography, 5G/6G, low-power wireless communications.

Applications & Testbeds

Intelligent healthcare, remote patient monitoring, smart manufacturing, Industrial IoT, sustainable cities, mobility, automotive, critical infrastructures, benchmarks and datasets.

Ethical & Regulatory Aspects

Compliance, accountability and interdisciplinary perspectives on distributed intelligence in IoT.

Quality Assessment

Non-invasive techniques for real-time quality monitoring, reliability assessment methods, metrics and benchmarking frameworks, digital twins for simulating and improving IoT system behaviour.

Quality Improvement & Testing

Software quality assurance for IoT, automated testing and debugging of ML-based systems, AI-based tools for self-adaptive software optimization, quality enhancement from operational feedback.
